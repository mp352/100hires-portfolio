* **Topic:** The 4 Tiers of AI-Generated Outbound Emails
* **Date Collected:** June 20, 2026

---

### 📝 Core Post Content

> "Last week, I explained to the Sell Better audience that I think AI generated emails fit into 4 tiers. Use these buckets to brainstorm how it could apply to your business.
>
> * **Top Tier:** The famous Jordan Crawford 'Permissionless Value Prop' email. An email so good, they would pay to receive it. Uses internal data, public scraped data, and AI to uncover a specific problem and give them a taste of the solution.
> * **Second Tier:** Based on what you would manually research about a company for 10 minutes, but automated using AI. Use literal messages you sent manually as training data for the AI.
> * **Third Tier:** Using AI to help someone imagine what it would be like if you were to work together. A simple one-liner adding specificity about what you do and how it specifically helps them.
> * **Fourth Tier:** Attention Hacking. Bottom of the barrel, often stolen from LinkedIn, but still works in unsophisticated industries (e.g., emailing local businesses and name-dropping the top steakhouse in the area to increase replies).
>
> When making AI outbound workflows in Clay: First, think if you can send something inherently valuable and custom. Second, what would you say manually? Third, use AI to make it specific based on their website."

---

### 💡 Key Takeaways & Practical Insight for B2B SaaS
* **The Blueprint for AI Workflows:** Eric outlines how to scale outbound without losing quality. Instead of generic AI writing, companies should use tools like Clay to enrich data first, then use AI as a personalization layer.
* **Training AI with Human Data:** Tier 2 highlights a golden rule—good AI outreach requires using real, successful manual emails as the baseline training data.
