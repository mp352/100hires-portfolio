### 📝 Core Post Content
> "Last week, I explained to the Sell Better audience that I think AI generated emails fit into 4 tiers. Use these buckets to brainstorm how it could apply to your business.
> 
> * **Top Tier:** The famous Jordan Crawford Permissionless Value Prop email. An email so good, they would pay to receive it. Using your own internal data, data you scrape publicly, and AI to show you did your research, uncovered a problem, and gave them a taste of the solution.
> * **Second Tier:** An email based on what you would research about a company for 10 minutes manually and using AI to change your messaging based on that. Use the literal messages you sent manually as the training data for your AI.
> * **Third Tier:** Using AI to help someone imagine what it would be like if you were to work together. Basically a liner adding specificity about what you do and how it could specifically help them.
> * **Fourth Tier:** Attention Hacking. Bottom of the barrel you probably stole it from someone on LinkedIn... e.g., emailing local businesses and name dropping the top steakhouse in the area still increases replies over not doing it.
> 
> So when making AI outbound workflows in Clay or other places: First think - is there anything I can send them that's inherently valuable and custom to their business without having to speak to them that I can automate. Second - what would I say if I were reaching out manually anyway? Third - use AI to make the email more specific to their situation and business from information you can easily find on their website. Fourth - do what you gotta do haha."

---

### 💡 Key Takeaways for B2B SaaS
* **Data Enrichment Before Generation:** High-quality AI outbound requires robust data sourcing (internal + scraped) to uncover real pain points before prompting the AI layer.
* **Human-Driven Baseline:** To train effective outbound AI models (Tier 2), teams must use high-performing manual sales copy as the foundational seed data.
