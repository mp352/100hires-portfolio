## 📌 Post 1: The 4 Tiers of AI-Generated Outbound Emails

### 📝 Core Post Content
> "Last week, I explained to the Sell Better audience that I think AI generated emails fit into 4 tiers. Use these buckets to brainstorm how it could apply to your business.
>
> * **Top Tier:** The famous Jordan Crawford 'Permissionless Value Prop' email. Uses internal data, public scraped data, and AI to uncover a specific problem and give them a taste of the solution.
> * **Second Tier:** Based on what you would manually research about a company for 10 minutes, but automated using AI. Use literal messages you sent manually as training data for the AI.
> * **Third Tier:** Using AI to help someone imagine what it would be like if you were to work together. A simple one-liner adding specificity about what you do and how it specifically helps them.
> * **Fourth Tier:** Attention Hacking. Bottom of the barrel, often stolen from LinkedIn, but still works in unsophisticated industries (e.g., name-dropping the top steakhouse in the area to increase replies)."

### 💡 Key Takeaways for B2B SaaS
* **Training AI with Human Data:** Tier 2 highlights a golden rule—good AI outreach requires using real, successful manual emails as the baseline training data.
* **Data Enrichment First:** Instead of generic AI writing, companies should use tools like Clay to enrich data first, then use AI as a personalization layer.

## 📌 Post 2: Multi-Batch Email Deliverability Infrastructure & Domain Aging

### 📝 Core Post Content
> "We are switching formats so that we are diversified AND we can quite possibly age domains for 3 months before using them. Now we are setting up 4 batches of inboxes for customers. We first estimate how many emails they'll need to be sending per day so that each batch is capable of hitting it.
> 
> * **Batch 1:** Google inboxes.
> * **Batch 2:** Outlook inboxes provided by Hypertide.io.
> * **Batch 3:** A combination of both inboxes.
> * **Batch 4:** Another combination of both inboxes.
> 
> We are finding right now to get the best performance with Google and Outlook inboxes need to warm longer... we are not using all inboxes at the same time. As long as things are looking good the other inboxes just keep on warming and we won't touch them. As domain age becomes more important, we have warmer inboxes so that if we ever burn some we can regroup, restrategize, refresh copy, and launch from new inboxes even warmer than before."

### 💡 Key Takeaways for B2B SaaS
* **Horizontal Scaling & Risk Diversification:** Moving from massive single-source blasts to a structured 4-batch infrastructure (Google + Outlook hybrid) isolates delivery failures and prevents total campaign shutdowns.
* **Continuous Domain Warming (Warmup Pools):** Keeping reserve batches in an untouched, prolonged warming state (up to 3 months) ensures that the pipeline has immediate, highly-reputable backups ready if active domains get flagged or burned.
